Board

<mh-board> is a kanban pipeline — fixed-width lanes side by side that scroll horizontally as a set. Each lane is a <section> with a <header> (stage name + count) and a stack of <mh-card> deal cards. Unlike mh-grid (cells wrap and reflow), a board keeps its columns in a row and scrolls when there are more stages than fit. The cards are just reused mh-card, tighter.

Example

Anatomy

A lane is a native <section> (a board column is a thematic grouping); its <header> carries the stage name and a <small> count. Deal cards are <mh-card> — title in the <header>, value in a <p>, owner <mh-avatar> + <mh-badge> in the <footer>. More lanes than fit → the board scrolls sideways.

Usage

<mh-board>
  <section>
    <header>Lead <small>3 · $61k</small></header>
    <mh-card>
      <header>Bletchley Co</header>
      <p>$12k · 15 min intro</p>
      <footer><mh-avatar>🧔</mh-avatar><mh-badge>🟡 New</mh-badge></footer>
    </mh-card>
    ...
  </section>
  ...
</mh-board>

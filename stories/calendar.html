Calendar

<mh-calendar> is a month grid built on a real <table> — a weekday <th> header row plus week rows of day <td>. The table keeps the calendar accessible and the weekday/column association free; the composite overrides the data-table look into equal day cells. Each cell holds a <strong> (the date) and <a> event chips. This is what mh-grid (wide auto-fit tracks) and the plain data <table> (left-aligned rows) can't be: a fixed 7-column day grid.

Example

Anatomy

Today is marked with aria-current="date" on its <td> — a native, self-describing signal, never a class. Out-of-month days are just empty <td>. Event chips are <a>; give one a commandfor="id" to open an event <dialog>.

Usage

<mh-calendar>
  <table>
    <thead><tr><th>Mon</th>…<th>Sun</th></tr></thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>1</strong></td>
        <td aria-current="date"><strong>2</strong><a commandfor="event">9:00 Standup</a></td>
        ...
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</mh-calendar>
